Coefficient of Thermal Expansion


The most common property measured on a TMA is the coefficient of thermal expansion (CTE) per international standards documented in ASTM E831, D969, D3380 and ISO 11359 Parts 1-3. The CTE describes the mechanical expansion or contraction of a material at different temperatures. It is an important property of a material, and neglecting to take into account the effect temperature has on the physical size of materials has been known to cause product failures and delamination.
