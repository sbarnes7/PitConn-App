Redox Reaction of Copper Oxalate 

Copper oxalate (CuC 2 O 4) is a salt that decomposes to elemental copper upon heating in an inert atmosphere. It is often used to measure the inertness of the TGA atmosphere, as the high surface area of copper is readily oxidized at high temperatures. In this example an oxidation-reduction (redox) reaction experiment was accomplished using the Blending GDM, Discovery MS and the Discovery TGA. After decomposition of the oxalate during the initial temperature ramp, oxygen was introduced into the TGA sample chamber resulting in the formation of copper (II) oxide. Subsequent reduction of the copper oxide was achieved through the introduction of small amounts of hydrogen gas. Forming gas was safely used as the hydrogen source in the TGA.
