The SDT 650 simultaneous DSC / TGA instrument captures both heat flow and weight change data in a single run, thus providing important information without concern over running a sample twice under the same conditions. The example here show calcium oxalate monohydrate run in both air and nitrogen. The weight data shows three weight loss transitions. The first is the loss of water. The second is the release of carbon monoxide as the sample decomposes to calcium carbonate. And the third is the release of carbon monoxide as the sample decomposes to calcium oxide. It is noted that when the sample is run in air that the second weight loss transition occurs slightly earlier than when run in nitrogen. Analysis of the heat flow data indicates that in nitrogen this transition is endothermic, while in air it is exothermic. The explanation is associated with the conversion of the released carbon monoxide to carbon dioxide through the presence of oxygen in the air. This is an exothermic transition and of such magnitude and proximity to the sensor that it is detected.
