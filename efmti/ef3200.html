ELECTROFORCE 3200

The industry’s highest-performing and most versatile table-top test instrument.



         



The ElectroForce® 3200 Series III test instruments feature a 225 N (450 N optional) maximum force. With the versatility of static to 300 Hz frequency response, the table-top configuration is adaptable to a variety of biomedical research and engineered materials test applications, including torsion testing, creep under dynamic loading and special environments (hot/cold chambers).

Features and Benefits:

  • Compact and portable
  • Monotonic to 300 Hz frequency response
  • Simple test space adjustment
  • Optional saline bath for testing at 37C
  • Well-suited for low force testing of biomaterials and engineered materials

3200 Series III Extended Stroke
The ElectroForce 3200 ES instrument combines two electromagnetic motor technologies to increase the range of displacement by 150 mm.

3200 Series III Axial/Torsion
The addition of a torsion (rotation) motor extends the flexibility of the 3200. The linear and torsional motors can be operated independently or synchronized to apply complex specimen loading. Independent magnetic designs offer the maximum performance by avoiding both inertia coupling and additional bearings.

The torsion motor includes a HADS (High Accuracy Displacement Sensor) for rotation measurements with resolution of 0.000005 degrees over a measurement range of 62 rotations. This configuration also includes a force-torque sensor for specimen load measurements. Users can choose to control rotation (angle) or torque with this motor, using WinTest’s TuneIQ® automatic tuning and control algorithms.

3200 Series III Multi-specimen Fatigue
Multi-specimen testing for fatigue life and failure detection through individual load measurements

3200 Series III BioDynamic
Sterilizable BioDynamic chambers for live tissue characterization

DMA 3200 High Force DMA
A special configuration of the 3200 instrument, focused on combining the dynamic strengths of the 3200 and delivering excellence in both DMA and fatigue studies.

Compression Testing of Cartilage

Researchers at the University of Minnesota, under the guidance of Dr. Jack Lewis, have been reviewing the issues of characterizing the mechanical properties of articular cartilage. In order to quantify the properties, cartilage must be tested in a unique dynamic loading system capable of providing precision creep capabilities while also providing a dynamic loading component during the same cycle. The ElectroForce® 3200 test instrument provides the capabilities required in a compact tabletop package.  Similar results could be obtained with other ElectroForce instruments such as models 3100, 5500 and 3300.

Cartilage tissue in vivo is under a constant compressive load with superimposed dynamic loads.  Therefore, specimen preparation and preloading are important aspects of in vitro test methods because articular cartilage has both a short time viscoelastic behavior and a long time constant for fluid retention.  

The plots show both cartilage response in both creep and relaxation tests and different loading rates, effectively modeling the material properties and characterizing the tissues under various conditions.
